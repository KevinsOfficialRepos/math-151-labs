Lyndon B. Johnson (LBJ)
The 1960s Reform Era

War On Poverty
    Economic Opportunity Act of 1964
    Poverty Line
        - Established official measure of poverty
        - Used to determine eligibility for federal assistance
    Job Corps
        - Training program for young people
        - Provided vocational skills and education
        - Residential program still active today
    VISTA (Volunteers In Service To America)
        - Domestic version of Peace Corps
        - Fought poverty through community service
        - Now part of AmeriCorps
    Food Stamps (SNAP)
        - Provided food assistance to low-income families
        - Made permanent in 1964
        - Now serves millions of Americans

Civil Rights
    1964 Civil Rights Act
        - Banned segregation in public places
        - Gave federal government power to enforce desegregation
        - One of the most significant civil rights laws in US history
        - Banned discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin
    Title VII
        - Prohibited employment discrimination
        - Required equal employment opportunities
        - Created basis for workplace anti-discrimination laws
    EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission)
        - Federal agency to enforce civil rights laws
        - Investigates discrimination complaints
        - Ensures workplace compliance with anti-discrimination laws
    Affirmative Action
        - Programs to increase opportunities for minorities
        - Applied to education and employment
        - Controversial but significant policy
    Voting Rights Act, 1965
        - Outlawed discriminatory voting practices 
        - Required federal preclearance in certain states
        - Shelby County v. Holder (2013) weakened federal oversight
    24th Amendment
        - Ratified 1964
        - Banned poll taxes in federal elections
        - Harper v. Virginia (1966) extended ban to state elections
    Age Discrimination in Employment Act 1967
        - Protected workers 40 and older
        - Prohibited age-based discrimination
        - Applied to hiring, promotion, and termination
    Immigration Act 1965
        - Ended national origins quota system
        - Established new immigration priorities
        - Changed US demographic patterns
Goldwater and the Election of 1964
    - Convinces LBJ is a socialist and wins presidentail nomination in 1966
    - LBJ's attack ad, 1964 (daisy attack)
Great Society: Education Reform
    Primary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA, 1965)
        - First major federal aid to education
        - Provided funding to schools with high numbers of low-income students
        - Started Title I programs still in use today
    Head Start (1965)
        - Early childhood education program for low-income families
        - Provided summer school programs
        - Focused on reading, writing, and basic skills
        - Helped level the educational playing field for disadvantaged children
    NEH, NEA (National Endowments for Humanities and Arts)
        - Created in 1965 to support cultural development
        - Provided grants for artists and scholars
        - Supported public cultural programs
    PBS NPR (Public Broadcasting Service, National Public Radio)
        - Created educational, non-commercial broadcasting networks
        - Focused on educational and cultural programming
        - Made quality programming accessible to all Americans
    Work-Study Program
        - Helped college students earn money while studying
        - Created part-time jobs for students with financial need
        - Still exists as Federal Work-Study program today
    College Student Loans
        - Established federal student loan programs
        - Made higher education more accessible
        - Created foundation for modern student loan system
    Medicare
        - Created by the Social Security Amendments of 1965
        - Federal health insurance for Americans 65+ and certain disabled groups
        - Covers hospital insurance (Part A) and supplementary medical benefits (Part B); core driver of senior health coverage

    Medicaid
        - Joint federal‑state program established in 1965
        - Provides health coverage for low‑income individuals and families; eligibility and benefits vary by state
        - Pays for a large share of long‑term care and maternal/child health services for needy populations

    Medical Research
        - Federal funding for biomedical research expanded (notably via the National Institutes of Health)
        - Supported research on cancer, heart disease, vaccines, and public health initiatives
        - Strengthened link between government funding and advances in medical knowledge and treatments

Watts
    - Watts riots (Los Angeles, 1965) exposed deep racial inequality, unemployment, poor housing, and policing tensions
    - Sparked federal attention to urban poverty and led to increased funding for anti‑poverty and community programs

Model Cities
    - Launched in 1966 as part of the Great Society to coordinate urban redevelopment and social services
    - Aimed to involve communities in planning public housing, health, education, and employment programs
    - Saw mixed results: some improvements but criticized for bureaucracy and insufficient funding

    Immigration Act
        - Refers to the Immigration and Nationality Act of 1965
        - Abolished the old national‑origins quota system, prioritized family reunification and skilled immigrants
        - Significantly diversified U.S. immigration patterns and sources over subsequent decades

Conservation
    Carson
        - Rachel Carson’s Silent Spring (1962) exposed ecological harms of pesticides like DDT
        - Sparked public concern and policy responses on chemical regulation and ecosystem protection

    Air Pollution: Los Angeles, 1958
        - Severe smog episodes in LA and other cities highlighted public health risks from car and industrial emissions
        - Prompted local and state controls and demonstrated need for national air quality standards

    Gary, Indiana
        - Example of industrial air and water pollution from steel and manufacturing centers
        - Illustrated environmental and public‑health impacts on industrial communities

    Air and water quality
        - Growing federal focus on monitoring, regulating emissions, and cleaning waterways
        - Set the stage for major laws and programs to limit pollutants and improve public health
        Wilderness
            - Wilderness Act (1964) established legal protection for undeveloped federal lands
            - Created a system to preserve areas in a natural, untrammeled condition
        Wild and Scenic Rivers
            - Wild and Scenic Rivers Act (1968) protected selected rivers from damming and harmful development
            - Preserved scenic, recreational, and ecological values of free‑flowing waterways

    EPA during Nixon
        - Nixon created the Environmental Protection Agency in 1970 to centralize federal environmental enforcement
        - EPA implemented and enforced Clean Air Act standards, water pollution controls, and later toxic substance regulation

National Park
    Guadalupe Mountains NP
        - Designated a national park in 1966 (Texas) to protect ancient reef formations, canyons, and high‑desert ecosystems
        - Expanded the national park system’s representation of desert and fossil reef landscapes

LBJ
    Population
        - Rapid postwar population growth and demographic shifts influenced housing, education, and urban policy planning
        - Policymaking addressed needs of growing Sunbelt and suburban populations as well as urban migration

    Consumer Protection
        - LBJ supported stronger consumer rights and government oversight of product safety and truthful marketing
        - Advocated measures to inform and protect buyers from deceptive or unsafe products

    Truth‑in‑Lending
        - Truth in Lending Act (1968) required creditors to disclose key loan terms, finance charges, and APR
        - Increased transparency in credit transactions to protect consumers from hidden costs and predatory lending

    Fair Packaging and Labeling
        - Fair Packaging and Labeling Act (1966) mandated clear, accurate labeling of consumer products
        - Helped consumers compare products and prevented deceptive packaging and claims
