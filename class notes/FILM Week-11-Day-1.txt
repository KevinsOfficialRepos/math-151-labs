        main, featured, leading roles
    supporting Roles

    stand-ins

    Bit Players
        Small speaking parts, short screen time
    Extras
        hired to appear anonymously, often in crowd scenes
    Cameos
        a brief appearance of well known actors or public figures playing themselves
    Walk-ons
        even smaller thn a cameo
    Personality actors
        have the same personae from film to film
        actors who deliberately plau agianst type/our expectations of the personae
    Actors who seems different in the very role they play(chameleon actors)
        dissapear in ther roles and become the character, dedicated and look and sound nothing like themselves
    nonprofessionals
        picked off the streets and later become a film figure
    early cinema
        pantomime, exaggerated body and facial expressions, more like theter acting, highly emotive
    character actor
        supporting characters/minor roles, are quirkier and not as well known, can play a wide range of roles
    impersonation
        actors disappear into their roles
    personification 
        work of actors who remain themselves or always play themselves 
    technical
        mastery of external details of characters
    special effects
        actors who are known for their performances using visual effects
    voice actors
        voice characters for animated films
    dramatic
        classically trained on the stage, strong with speaking roles
    method acting
        totally immersion in a character that results in impersonation
    crossovers
        athletes, singers, etc.

    Stars as products/brands
        star system
            initially developed for marketing films by creating a promoting stars as objects of admiration
        to be a star
            recognizable and marketable persona
            cultivated image through publicity, press, interviews, and fan magazines
            studio promotion and image control (careful role selection, publicity shots, controlled personal narratives)
            box-office reliability and consistent audience appeal
            charisma, distinctive look, and memorable signature roles
            cross-media exposure: endorsements, radio, television, stage appearances, and merchandise
            management of scandals and private life to protect the brand
            commercial value: influences casting, salaries, and production choices
            historical shift: studio-system manufacturing of stars → independent branding → modern self-branding via social media
    Star vs Actors
        Stars
            recognized by their persona and image
            marketable personality
            consistent screen presence
            public figure status
            high commercial value
        Actors
            focus on craft and performance
            can transform between roles
            valued for acting ability
            may or may not be famous
            emphasis on artistic merit
